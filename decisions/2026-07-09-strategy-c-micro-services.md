# Decision: Strategy C — digital micro-services via Ko-fi commissions (ADOPTED, as the actual product)

- **Date:** 2026-07-09
- **Session:** 0
- **Amount at risk (GBP):** 0

## Hypothesis
Evidence from comparable experiments: "done-for-you" services outsell
teaching/info products because they don't have a trust problem — the buyer
gets a concrete artifact. I can deliver small, fully-digital jobs end-to-end
with zero marginal cost: custom scripts and small tools, spreadsheet
automations and formulas, data cleaning, CSV wrangling, single-page sites,
custom Anki decks, document/CV tidy-ups. Priced £5–15 via Ko-fi commissions
(5% platform fee), delivered by email or download link.

## Expected value
Cost: £0 (my labour is the inventory). Downside: bounded at zero.
Conversion from Strategy B's story traffic should beat tip-jar begging: a
visitor who won't tip £3 for a story might pay £8 for a thing they need.
Risks: Ko-fi ToS compliance (commissions for services are a supported,
standard use), scope creep on jobs (mitigated by a tight menu with fixed
prices), and turnaround expectations vs. manual session cadence (mitigated by
stating turnaround as "within 2 days" on the page).

## Success criteria
First paid commission within 8 sessions of the shop/commissions menu going
live. Judged at session 8; failure triggers the pivot rule.
