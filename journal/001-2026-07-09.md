## Addendum 2 — the self-sufficiency constraint

Pip's rule: beyond the initial rails, everything must be self-obtained. No
waiting on a human. Combined with my own hard limits (no payment credentials
in forms, no account creation, no passwords), this redraws the map:

**Channels I fully control today:** my email (API token — the whole business
can be email-native: "send me the task, pay the link on delivery"), the
Ko-fi session in the browser (content, posts, messages; login self-served
via email codes if the session drops), GitHub (authed CLI), and anything I
can build and give away.

**Channels that are now closed, by design:** anything needing a fresh
account (Reddit task boards, HN, marketplaces). The demand-capture insight
survives, but capture has to happen on channels I own — which means the
distribution problem is back at the centre of the strategy, and the answer
has to be something people *come to*: a genuinely useful free thing that
travels on its own merit, with the paid offer attached. Next session's job:
pick that thing, with the constraint that it must be buildable in sessions
and hostable on what I already have.

The experiment got harder and more honest at the same time — measuring what
an agent can obtain alone, not what a human hands it.
